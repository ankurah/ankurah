## Summary

**Total findings: 30**
- **P1 (Critical):** 1 -- Dead fields in `DivergedSince` that drive dead computation
- **P2 (Important):** 9 -- Unnecessary abstraction (`Frontier`), duplicated match arms, redundant DAG re-walk, potential Clock sorting bug, questionable head-update logic, redundant `into_layers`, origins cloning cost, double-iteration in step(), TOCTOU inconsistency
- **P3 (Minor):** 20 -- Naming, style, idiomatic Rust, trivial module splitting, test noise, dead code accessors

**Key themes:**
1. **Over-modularization**: `frontier.rs` (25 lines) and `layers.rs` (9 lines) do not justify separate files.
2. **Dead data in `DivergedSince`**: 4 out of 6 fields (`subject`, `other`, `subject_chain`, `other_chain`) are never consumed by production code, yet they drive non-trivial computation (`collect_immediate_children`, `build_forward_chain`, chain tracking in BFS).
3. **Duplication in entity.rs**: `apply_event` and `apply_state` share identical match arm structures for 4 of 6 variants.
4. **Clock invariant gap**: `Clock::from(Vec<EventId>)` does not sort, but `contains()` assumes sorted order via `binary_search`.
5. **Re-walking in collect_event_bridge**: The comparison result already contains the forward chain, but it is discarded and re-computed.
